AutoAnn-Indic: Human-Effort Efficient Automated Annotation for Indic Manuscripts and Ram Charit Manas

A practice-oriented challenge focused on scalable, high-quality automated pre-annotation for Indic manuscript pages, evaluated not only by annotation quality but also by measurable human correction effort.

1) Introduction

Indic manuscripts (paper and palm-leaf) present extreme variability in scripts, calligraphic styles, ink density, textures, geometry, and degradations (bleed-through, stains, cracks, holes, fading, ornaments, and marginalia). Although digitization has produced large image collections, high-quality annotation for layout, baselines, damage, and reading order remains a bottleneck.

AutoAnn-Indic addresses this with an automated annotation generation challenge using a diverse Indic manuscript corpus and a focused Ram Charit Manas subset. Participants will receive a curated page set, small seed labels, and a larger unlabeled pool to encourage weak/self/semi-supervised learning and active learning.

Submissions will include layout regions (text, marginalia/notes, illustrations/diagrams, page/frame, damage/holes) and text-line/baseline outputs in standard formats (PAGE-XML and/or COCO schema as announced).

2) Impact & Significance

Research impact

  • Beyond accuracy-only benchmarks: evaluates quality plus human effort saved.
  • Low-label learning at scale: supports weak supervision, self-training, and active learning.
  • Robustness under realistic degradations: targets hard manuscript conditions and domain shifts.
  • Downstream improvements: benefits OCR/HTR, retrieval, reading order, and translation workflows.

Societal & ecosystem impact

  • Faster and lower-cost digitization workflows for archives and libraries.
  • Improved preservation and discovery of Indic cultural heritage content.
  • Release of reusable formats, scripts, and annotation guidelines for the community.

3) Major Rules for Participation & Evaluation Criteria

A) Participation rules

  1. Data policy: train only on the officially released AutoAnn-Indic dataset.
  2. External pretraining: allowed with full disclosure of model/weights/source/data.
  3. No extra manual labeling: additional human annotation beyond seed labels is prohibited.
  4. Submission format: official schema + page IDs, using PAGE-XML and/or COCO-style JSON.
  5. Submission limits: test submissions capped to prevent overfitting (e.g., 3–5 per team).
  6. Reproducibility: shortlisted teams must provide method summary and runnable inference.
  7. Ethics & fair use: research-only use, licensing compliance, no harmful content.

B) Evaluation criteria

The leaderboard combines annotation quality (Q) and human effort (E).

  • Track 1 (Layout): mIoU / mask mAP, with PQ where applicable.
  • Track 2 (Lines/Baselines): Line IoU / F1 and tolerance-based baseline metrics.
  • Human effort: median correction time (seconds/page) on a hidden subset under fixed protocol.

Final ranking may use Pareto ranking or a combined score like S = Q × exp(-λE) (or an equivalent normalized weighted combination), with tie-breakers on hard-subset performance, lower effort, and optional runtime.

4) Novelty of the Challenge

  • Auto-annotation first: practical generation of pre-annotations for scalable label creation.
  • Human-in-the-loop benchmark: explicit quality–cost objective with correction-time measurement.
  • Realistic low-label setup: seed labels + unlabeled pool across Indic + Ram Charit Manas domains.

This design rewards models that are both accurate and efficient for real annotation workflows.
